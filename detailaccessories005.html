Frequently Asked Questions

Thread by Steelcase Power hubs are lightweight and portable, so users can bring power to any space, when and where it’s needed.

- Available in both lounge and desk height to accommodate different furniture heights

- Power hubs manage and store 5’ cords, making it easy to place power precisely where it’s needed

- There is a breaker included in the base to prevent a power surge

- Provides users with 6 outlets per hub

- Lightweight and portable

  • What is Steelcase Thread?

    Thread is a simpler way to provide power to the devices we use and the places where we use them. As a freestanding power hub, Thread allows you to add additional power outlets to rooms that need them without the need for expensive renovations to the room by allowing you to use the power infrastructure your spaces already have.

  • What is a power hub?

    A power hub is a device that allows for the easy recharging and powering of electronics ranging from phones and tablets to laptops. Steelcase Thread provides six standard outlets per hub, as well as the ability to manage and store 5’ cords to place power exactly where it’s needed.

  • How is Thread installed?

    Thread requires a single power source to function, either from a floor infeed or a wall infeed depending on where you want Thread to be connected. From there, Thread’s ultrathin design can be installed underneath your existing carpet or floor coverings to hide the cable track, and the freestanding power hubs can be placed anywhere in the room.

  • Can you use an HDMI cable with Thread?

    Thread is only compatible with power cables, as it is built for charging and powering devices. However, Thread power hubs are compatible with any device that uses standard two- or three-prong power cables.
