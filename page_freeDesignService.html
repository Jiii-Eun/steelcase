Bobby Berk + Steelcase Design Services

  • Bobby Berk is best known as the beloved and inspiring interior designer on Netflix’s reality makeover show Queer Eye. Off camera, he runs a full-service interior design and creative firm. The team’s latest design challenge was the renovation of an entire L.A. house into a fully functioning workplace that serves as the firm’s headquarters. Steelcase Design Services was thrilled to collaborate on the project with Bobby Berk and team and design solutions that addressed the genuine needs of the workspace.

    Berk wanted to create an office everyone could enjoy, feel comfortable and thrive in — where people are empowered to be themselves and bring what makes them unique into the office every day. Every team member was invited to provide input on the design which included asking how they like to work.

    The Steelcase design team looked at layout, materials, colors and specific furniture pieces that would function best for each of the dynamic workspaces in the house. Design solutions were presented based on a clear understanding of how Berk's team would utilize each space for work and collaboration.

  • We always co-create with our clients. It’s important for us to establish a trusting relationship,” said Amanda VanDuyn, Steelcase senior interior designer. “Bobby’s design team came to us with great ideas and an inspiring vision. We were able to put a spin on some of their ideas and provide them with creative options for showcasing their space in a different way,” she added.

    Going for an organic modern vibe, with clean lines, a minimal palette and earthy touches, Steelcase Design Services helped hone in their palette, look and feel. The team provided Berk’s team with full renderings of their spaces so they could see how their ideas could come to life.

    Steelcase suggested furniture pieces from our portfolio of brands that would support the team’s daily work needs. After sharing overall design plans and material choices, they selected pieces that would seamlessly integrate and function well in the space.

  • “We wanted pieces that didn’t feel too ‘officey,’ that felt homey, but would stand up over time,” said Brady Tolbert, creative director at Bobby Berk. “The office chairs we chose, for example, are so sleek and simple. They feel like the most luxurious office chairs without looking like office chairs.”

    For the entire project, Berk kept his design mission at heart, “to help everyone discover that good design is good for the mind,” Berk says. “We want to design spaces that make you feel good when you’re there.”

    Likewise, Steelcase is all about creating highly functional workspaces that help you work better, be your most productive and creative, and feel good all day.

    Years in the making, the team’s new “home office” is giving them everything they need to get great work done together and alone, connect with customers and partners, and feel good at the same time. Steelcase Design Services can help you design the office of your dreams.
