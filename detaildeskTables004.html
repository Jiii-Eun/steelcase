  • How can I organize a desk without drawers?

    Many desks don’t offer as much storage space due to their streamlined design. If your desk needs a little help organizing smaller things like cables and writing supplies, a desk organizer can be the perfect solution. Desk organizers are ideal for any desk with extra surface up top but not as many drawers underneath, allowing you to keep the tools you need every day close at hand.

  • What are desk organizers used for?

    Desk organizers are best used for any desk that needs a little extra storage space, or if your work requires the use of a lot of smaller tools (pens, pencils, digital drawing pens/tablets) that could be easily lost or create a lot of clutter on your desk. Simply place the organizer somewhere on your desk where it won’t get in the way of your mouse and keyboard, and use it to store all your paperwork and office supplies in an easy-to-access fashion!

  • How can I make my laptop higher on my desk?

    Raising your laptop is easy with a laptop shelf. Place the shelf on your desk in the usual spot where you keep your laptop (close to any adjacent docks, extra monitors, etc) and use it to raise your monitor to a more comfortable height. The use of laptop shelves can reduce neck strain over time and make for a better sitting and working experience overall.

  • Should you use a footrest at your desk?

    Using a footrest underneath your desk allows you to keep a more neutral position throughout the day, which could improve your posture, reduce muscle strain, and improve your overall ergonomics.

  • How to use a footrest at my desk?

    Using a footrest at your desk is simply a matter of good positioning. Place the footrest under your desk at a position that places it underneath your feet, but still out in front of you, allowing you to rest comfortably on the footrest while leaning back into your chair. This will help you maintain a better posture and reduce strain on your legs and lower back.

  • How to use a cable organizer on my desk?

    In order to best use cable organizers, you should start by unplugging everything at your desk that you want to run through the cable organizer. From there, you can re-thread the cables from their starting point (typically the power outlet, or the computer tower/dock in the case of cables for mice and keyboards) through the cable organizer, before plugging them back in. This way, you can avoid tangles or confusion, while your new cable organizer helps keep the cables off your desk, and off the floor!
